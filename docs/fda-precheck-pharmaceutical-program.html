FDA Launches PreCheck Pilot Program to Strengthen Domestic Pharmaceutical Manufacturing

Published: February 2, 2026 12:15 PM PT | Source: FDA Press Release (February 1, 2026)

Breaking News: FDA Begins Accepting Applications for New Pharmaceutical Manufacturing Initiative

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has officially launched its PreCheck pilot program designed to strengthen domestic pharmaceutical manufacturing and supply chains. As of February 1, 2026, the agency has begun accepting requests from pharmaceutical manufacturers to participate in this groundbreaking initiative.

Program Details

The FDA PreCheck program aims to address several critical challenges in the pharmaceutical supply chain by:

  • Increasing regulatory predictability for manufacturers
  • Facilitating the construction of new manufacturing sites in the United States
  • Streamlining aspects of pharmaceutical manufacturing facility assessments before specific product applications

According to the FDA announcement, an initial cohort of new pharmaceutical manufacturing facilities will be selected to begin PreCheck activities in 2026. The selection process will prioritize facilities based on alignment with national priorities across multiple criteria:

  • Types of products to be manufactured
  • Phase of facility development
  • Timeline to producing pharmaceutical products for the U.S. market
  • Innovation in facility development

Special priority consideration will be given to facilities planning to produce critical medications for the U.S. market, addressing ongoing concerns about supply chain resilience for essential pharmaceuticals.

Two-Phase Implementation

The FDA PreCheck program consists of two distinct phases:

Phase 1: Facility Readiness

In this initial phase, selected manufacturers will engage with FDA for early technical advice before a facility becomes operational. This includes:

  • Pre-operational reviews
  • Utilization of a facility-specific Drug Master File

These steps are designed to facilitate efficient evaluation of facility-specific elements prior to, and in support of, the submission of a drug application.

Phase 2: Application Submission

Building on Phase 1, the second phase involves:

  • Pre-submission meetings between FDA and applicants
  • Targeted inspections to resolve issues
  • Expedited assessments of manufacturing information in drug applications

Industry Input

The FDA incorporated extensive industry feedback into the program design based on:

  • Comments from the "Onshoring Manufacturing of Drugs and Biological Products" public meeting held on September 30, 2025
  • Public comments received through Federal Register publication

According to the FDA, industry stakeholders strongly supported early engagement during facility development and streamlined documentation processes, both of which are central elements of the PreCheck program.

Market Implications

This initiative represents a significant step toward addressing pharmaceutical supply chain vulnerabilities exposed during global disruptions in recent years. By incentivizing domestic manufacturing through regulatory streamlining, the program aims to enhance the resilience and security of America's pharmaceutical supply.

The launch of the FDA PreCheck program could have significant implications for pharmaceutical manufacturers, particularly those considering new U.S.-based facilities. Companies that successfully participate in the pilot may gain competitive advantages through accelerated approval timelines and reduced regulatory uncertainty.

This development may also influence investment decisions in the pharmaceutical manufacturing sector, potentially accelerating the trend toward reshoring critical drug production capabilities.
